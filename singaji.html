Biografy

Singaji was born in village Khajuri district Barwani in Madhya Pradesh. It is named after him. Singaji is a small village in Harsud tehsil, 28 miles northeast of Khandwa in Madhya Pradesh. Harsud was built by Harshavardhana. It was in this village that Singaji was born into an ordinary Gawli family. Inspired by Singaji's talent in the 16th century, the village zamindar appointed him as their chieftain. Singaji served the zamindar for twelve years and also won many battles for the zamindar with his spiritual enchanting powers. According to some sources, he also worked as a letter bearer to the king of Bhamagarh. But in time he took sannyas and became an ascetic. At the age of 40, he took samadhi.

Widespread Recognition

Saint Singaji is believed to date back to the 16th or 17th century. It is said that Singaji was a poet and enchanting saint. Singaji's deras and samadhis are also held at many places throughout Madhya Pradesh, where fairs are also organized. The spiritual songs of the poet Saint Singaji are sung to this day.

Sant Singaji Reservoir Project

Sant Singaji Reservoir is also part of the Narmada Sagar Project developed on the Narmada river by the Narmada Hydropower Development Corporation, which is spread over 913 square meters. It is the largest reservoir in India and the second largest in Asia. The sacred tomb of Singaji has also been constructed here. The estimated cost of the Singaji project is Rs 5 crore.

Sharad Purnima and Mela

The festival of Sant Singaji in village Pipliya-Singaji, near Beer village of Khandwa, starts from Sharad Purnima (Sharad Purnima) in the month of October-November. During this event, more than two and a half million devotees visit the holy tomb of Singaji Maharaj. Nimar's culture and tradition is seen in this fair. That's why this fair has made its identity abroad. In this fair, as a symbol of faith of Nimar, devotees from other districts and states, including Jhabua, Badwani, Betul, Khargone, and Maharashtra also visit. Ghee, coconut, chironji are mainly offered on Singaji Samadhi. The devotees who fulfill their vows also do Bhandara here. Sant Singaji is also known as Kabir of Nimar region. Even today, his footprints are worshiped at Nimar’s place of birth and samadhi. People still feel his miracles. He was a great figure of Narmadanchal. Farmers offer him animal milk and ghee. In these places, the Akhand Jyot (continuously lighted lamp) has been burning for centuries.
